Dr. A. P. J.Abdul Kalam

Born 15 October 1931 Rameswaram Died 27 July 2015 (aged 83)

"If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun"

Biography

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the "Missile Man of India," was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Here are some key points about his life and contributions:
1. Early Life and Education: Dr. Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He came from a humble background and faced financial constraints during his early life. He studied aerospace engineering and graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology.
2. ISRO Career: He started his career at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in the 1960s. He played a pivotal role in the development of India's first satellite, Rohini, and the successful launch of SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle).
3. Role in India's Missile Program: Dr. Kalam's most significant contributions were in the field of defense technology. He played a crucial role in the development of India's Agni and Prithvi missiles, which enhanced the country's defense capabilities.
4. Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: He was closely associated with the successful conduct of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear-armed nation.
5. Awards and Honors: Dr. Kalam received numerous awards and honors throughout his life, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997. He was also awarded the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan.
6. Vision 2020: He had a vision for India to become a developed nation by 2020, which he outlined in his book, "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium." He emphasized the importance of education and technology in achieving this vision.
7. Presidential Term: Dr. Kalam served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. During his presidency, he continued to focus on education, particularly on inspiring the youth to pursue science and innovation.
8. Role as an Educator: After his term as President, he returned to his passion for teaching and engaging with students. He delivered lectures and interacted with young minds across India, encouraging them to pursue careers in science and technology.
9. Author: Dr. Kalam was a prolific author, and he wrote several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, and "Ignited Minds," which discussed the need for igniting the minds of the youth for a brighter future.
10. People's President: He was widely known as the "People's President" due to his humble and approachable nature. He continued to be an inspiration to millions of Indians, especially the youth, and remains a respected figure even after his passing in 2015.
